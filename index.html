Honours Project

Mixing AI, Quantum Security and Neuroscience


BCIs allow human brains to directly control or even communicate with machines, devices, and more. But the problem is, current BCI data can be used to literally read someone's mind—guess passwords, subliminally detect who you know, figure out your mood, and more.


This problem will only get more severe as in the future Quantum Computers will break encryption making all BCI recorded data vulnerable.


My solution uses Lattices, a mathematical structure similar to the ones on the graphic, to securely encrypt the data, even safely from Quantum Computers. The remarkable things about Lattices, is they allow for data to be processed, while encrypted, something that is impossible with traditional cryptography. Meaning that your vulnerable data, would never need to be decrypted (where its vulnerable) to be processed.


This property is called Homomorphic Cryptography.


The proposed paper's title:


Protecting Neural Data Privacy with Quantum-Safe CKKS Lattice-Based Homomorphic Encryption in Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning
