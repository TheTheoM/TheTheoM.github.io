Honours Project

Mixing AI, Quantum Security and Neuroscience


BCIs, or Brain Computer Interfaces, allow human brains to directly control or even communicate with machines, devices, and software. But the problem is, the data that BCI's record, if stolen, can be used to literally read someone's mind, allowing hackers to steal your passwords, figure out what people you know, your mood and much more.


This problem will only get more severe as in the future as Quantum Computers will break encryption making all BCI recorded data vulnerable to attack!


My approach uses Lattices to encrypt the data, in a way that Quantum Computers cannot break. (The graphic is an representation of a lattice). The key advantage of lattices is that they enable data to be processed while still encrypted, which is not possible with traditional cryptography. This means that your sensitive data never needs to be decrypted, reducing the risk of exposure during processing.


This ground-breaking property is called Homomorphic Cryptography.


The proposed paper's title:


Protecting Neural Data Privacy with Quantum-Safe CKKS Lattice-Based Homomorphic Encryption in Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning
